== The Translation

The Reina-Valera Bible is the Spanish equivalent of the King James Version in English. First translated by Casiodoro de Reina (1569), revised by Cipriano de Valera (1602), and updated through the centuries (most notably in 1960).

== Study Approach

This section documents study of Scripture as:

1. **Literature** — Poetry, narrative, wisdom literature
2. **History** — Ancient Near East, Israel, early church
3. **Theology** — Systematic understanding of faith
4. **Language** — Spanish vocabulary, idioms, phrasing
5. **Life** — Application and meditation
